Brazil's Conflict Landscape¶

by Jessica Ertel¶

Investigation Overview¶

The goal of this investigation is to better understand the nature of conflict in Brazil by examining conflict events that took place between Jan 1, 2019 and May 4, 2020. The main focus is fatality counts, locations and the category of the event.

Dataset Overview¶

This dataset was aquired via the Humanitarian Data Exchange (HDX) and shared by the Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project (ACLED). The dataset contains over 14,000 records, including characteristics about the type, agents, exact location, and date of the event.

Distribution of Fatalities¶

In this dataset, a total of 6,609 fatalities happened across 14,222 observations. Fortunately, a high number of events involved no fatalities, but approximately 11 had a fatality count over 8 people. Further investigation reveals that the high fatality events are mostly concentrated in the northern regions of Brazil.

Conflict Locations¶

Rio de Janeiro state leads by a large margin in events and fatalities.¶

Rio de Janeiro state not only has the highest number of records in the dataset; it also has the highest count of fatalities. In fact, the top 7 states with the most records in the dataset are also the states with the deadliest conflicts.

What kinds of conflict are taking place?¶

Exploring the types of conflicts happening in the states with the highest number of records reveals a similar breakdown. Minas Gerais and São Paulo's conflicts are largely peaceful protests, wheras Rio de Janeiro is predominantly armed clashes. Perhaps the large number of protests in São Paulo can be attributed to the fact that it houses Brazil's capital (it also had the lowest number of casualties in the dataset). Minas Gerais also sees a high number of protests and a relatively high number of conflicts involving violence against civilians.

Conclusions¶

This dataset evidences 14,222 conflict events in Brazil throughout 2019 and in the first trimester of 2020. 46.5% of these events were deadly - involving fatalities of one or more people. Rio de Janeiro state had by far the highest number of events and the highest number of fatalities in Brazil. While the Southeastern states of Rio de Janeiro, Minas Gerais and Sao Paulo had a high number of events, the states in Northeastern Brazil saw a higher number of fatalities. The geopolitical landscape plays a role here, but it was hard to get a sense of seasonality or trends in the timing of conflict events without a longer timeframe.
